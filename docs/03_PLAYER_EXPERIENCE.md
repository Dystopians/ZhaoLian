# 03 — Player Experience and User Stories

## Experience pillars

### P1 — Knowledge is uneven

Different people know different names, languages, and risks. Suspense comes from who knows what—not from hiding Zhao Lian's identity from an informed player.

### P2 — Editing is action

Choosing a noun, certainty marker, or blank is a meaningful gameplay act. A polished sentence can be ethically weaker than an incomplete one.

### P3 — Violence remains off-screen

The absence of a death scene is not a content omission; it is the form of the historical record and a deliberate design rule.

### P4 — Daily life carries the weight

Wine bottles, accounts, clothes, food cooling, a lamp wick, a hat not worn, and a child born should bear more emotional weight than melodramatic speeches.

### P5 — No moral score

`rigor`, `care`, and `closure` are hidden editorial tendencies, not virtue meters. Every ending has a legitimate social function and an ethical cost.

## Intended emotional curve

1. Curiosity about an ambiguous local businessman.
2. Unease about translation and collaboration.
3. Recognition of protective action inside compromise.
4. Shock contained within one change of address: “郁先生”.
5. Relief at surrender news.
6. Slow dread after an ordinary knock.
7. Powerlessness expressed through waiting.
8. Analytic tension while comparing statements.
9. Responsibility while writing the final record.
10. Reflection rather than cathartic solution.

## Core user stories

- **PX-001:** As a player unfamiliar with Yu Dafu, I can understand who Zhao Lian is without a pre-game biography lecture.
- **PX-002:** As a knowledgeable reader, I can see that the game is not pretending the historical identity is a surprise twist.
- **PX-003:** As a player, I can identify whether a detail is documented, remembered, inferred, or invented for staging.
- **PX-004:** As a keyboard-only player, I can complete every interaction.
- **PX-005:** As a screen-reader user, I can understand evidence strength and interface changes without relying on layout or color.
- **PX-006:** As a player sensitive to war violence, I can receive an accurate content notice and never encounter graphic death depiction.
- **PX-007:** As a player, I can leave a field blank without the interface treating me as incorrect.
- **PX-008:** As a returning player, I can choose a different observation path and produce a different archival text without searching for a secret canon ending.
- **PX-009:** As a teacher or curator, I can use the source/methodology screens to distinguish game reconstruction from historical claims.
- **PX-010:** As an Indonesian or Japanese reader, I am not asked to accept collective ethnic guilt in place of specific institutions and actors.

## Onboarding

The first five minutes must teach through action:

- a name can be entered or withheld;
- a detail can be observed but not all details at once;
- a claim has a material class;
- the dossier is available but optional;
- the game will not let the player change the historical outcome.

The explicit contract appears before the first historical reconstruction:

> 你不能改变这件事。你将改变它留下来的文字。

## Replay philosophy

Replay is for perspective and editorial comparison, not collectible truth. After the first completion:

- allow chapter restart from prologue only, preserving a separate completed-run archive;
- show previously encountered evidence in a muted “known from another run” collection, but do not let it count as observed evidence in the current report;
- never expose a percentage suggesting that all truth can be collected.
