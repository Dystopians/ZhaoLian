# 33 — Editorial Style Guide

## Language

- Primary text: Simplified Chinese.
- Use full-width Chinese punctuation in prose.
- Use Arabic numerals for precise archival dates and UI fields; Chinese numerals may appear in literary prose where natural.
- Keep names consistent with glossary, while intentionally varying Zhao/Yu according to social perspective.

## Sentence style

- Prefer concrete action over psychological explanation.
- Average sentence should remain readable on mobile; alternate short clauses with occasional longer archival analysis.
- Avoid decorative adjectives and repeated declarations of tragedy.
- Let ordinary objects carry emotion.
- Do not imitate Yu Dafu's first-person confessional style.

## Point of view

- Historical reconstruction: third-person external.
- Archival intervention: second person “你”.
- Appendix: neutral editorial prose with explicit attribution.
- Do not enter Zhao's mind after the threshold; ideally avoid unsupported private interiority throughout.

## Certainty language

Recommended:

- `材料显示……`
- `据胡愈之后来的记述……`
- `一名目击者称……`
- `后来调查提出……`
- `现有材料强烈指向……`
- `无法确认……`

Avoid unless source class supports it:

- `事实证明……`
- `毫无疑问……`
- `他一定知道……`
- `杀手就是……`
- `真正的结局……`

## Quotation

- Use Chinese quotation marks for sourced or clearly staged dialogue.
- Paraphrase is not placed in quotation marks.
- Reconstructed dialogue must be identified in source mode/methodology.
- Do not expand a short remembered phrase into a long speech.

## Naming

Use contextually:

- local/household/business scenes: `赵廉`, `赵老板`, or attested local form;
- fellow-exile/identity scene: `郁达夫`, `郁先生` where knowledge supports it;
- player report: chosen formulation.

Narrator should not casually switch names without purpose.

## Japanese institution terminology

Use a historian-reviewed term consistently. Avoid vague “日本人” when the responsible institution can be named; avoid attributing every Japanese civilian or soldier to the same role.

## Indonesian context

- Avoid “南洋小镇” as the only descriptor; use Payakumbuh/West Sumatra context.
- Avoid exoticizing heat, jungle, bodies, or accents.
- Use local names and language only after review.

## Interface copy

- Buttons begin with clear verbs or the exact utterance chosen.
- Errors explain what happened and what to do.
- Never use shame language for uncertainty.
- “不详”, “无法确认”, and blank are legitimate values, not failure.
