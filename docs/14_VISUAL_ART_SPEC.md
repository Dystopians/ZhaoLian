# 14 — Visual and Art Direction

## Visual thesis

An archival surface gradually admits the humidity, material life, and spatial uncertainty of West Sumatra. The work should feel handled, catalogued, and incomplete—not sepia nostalgia or tropical exoticism.

## Style

- Original restrained illustration, collage, or limited-detail environmental art.
- Documentary objects: ledger, bottle label, map, identity paper, report form, typewriter paper.
- People shown through partial framing, hands, silhouettes, backs, or non-photoreal portraits when identity is not the subject.
- Real photographs appear only in the post-game appendix with provenance and rights.
- Avoid visual imitation of a specific living artist.

## Palette goals

Provide accessible tokens with normal and high-contrast variants. Suggested emotional families, not immutable values:

- warm paper neutral;
- ink charcoal;
- humid green-gray;
- oxidized metal/brown;
- muted radio amber;
- warning accent that is never the sole signal.

All text/background combinations must pass required contrast.

## Required scene assets

1. Archive title desk.
2. Rainy wine-factory interior.
3. Military-police office and name list.
4. Night room/fan/lamp.
5. Wine-table identity-exposure scene.
6. Radio and practical future objects.
7. House doorway at night.
8. Dawn interior after waiting.
9. Coffee-shop vicinity.
10. Road and distant vehicle impression.
11. Split archival date comparison.
12. Final report desk.
13. Six ending-specific settlement illustrations:
    - memorial wording / monument surface;
    - balanced case-file archive;
    - household absence after departure;
    - untranslated blanks and preserved uncertainty;
    - cross-witness testimony comparison;
    - translation-delay suspicion and identity-risk shadow.

Ending settlement art may layer map fragments, doorframes, paper, waterlike light, night/dawn overlap, and negative space to suggest temporal interlacing. It must remain non-photographic and must not invent a death scene, corpse, grave coordinate, or solved location.

Optional supporting assets:

- map of Singapore–Sumatra movement with qualified labels;
- Payakumbuh town context;
- bottle labels “双清” / “初恋” only after historical/rights review;
- archive boxes and source cards.

## Character depiction rules

- Do not render the young caller as sinister by ethnicity, skin tone, or facial distortion.
- Do not create a heroic martyr portrait during the departure.
- Japanese military-police power should be communicated via uniform fragments, files, posture, vehicles, and access—not demonic faces.
- The wife must appear in active domestic or decision-making poses, not only in labor/childbirth imagery.

## Motion

Allowed:

- subtle paper shift;
- radio noise;
- light rain;
- lamp dimming;
- dossier transitions.

Forbidden:

- camera shake at the knock;
- horror flashes;
- simulated strangulation;
- rapid zoom on “郁先生”;
- parallax that impairs reading.

All motion has reduced-motion alternatives.

## Asset production states

- `BLOCKOUT`: simple geometric or licensed placeholder.
- `DRAFT`: original low-detail art, correct composition.
- `REVIEW`: historian/cultural/accessibility review.
- `FINAL`: optimized, attributed, alt text complete.

## Image rights

No hotlinked web image may ship. Reference images are research-only until rights are verified. Public-domain, appropriately licensed, commissioned, or original assets are preferred. See `34_ASSET_AND_LICENSE_POLICY.md`.
