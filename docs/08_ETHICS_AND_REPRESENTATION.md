# 08 — Ethics and Representation

## Ethical thesis

The work may use uncertainty as form, but it may not use uncertainty to erase responsibility or manufacture mystery for entertainment.

## Mandatory boundaries

- No playable execution, corpse, grave discovery, torture vignette, or death sound.
- No invented last thoughts, patriotic final speech, or knowingly walking to martyrdom.
- No implication that Zhao's death results from a player mistake.
- No “collaborator reveal” used as cheap reversal; ambiguity around interpreter work must be contextualized as coercion, cover, and constrained agency.
- No ethnic essentialism toward Japanese or Indonesian people. Name the Japanese military-police institution and occupation system.
- No collective blame assigned to the unnamed young caller's community.
- No framing of the wife as merely pregnant, passive, or symbolically useful.
- No collectible victims, rescue count, or moral score.
- No graphic content used in marketing.

## Wife and family requirements

The wife must:

- speak or act in at least three scenes;
- make at least two practical decisions;
- use or negotiate the name Zhao Lian independently of literary history;
- not be reduced to the moment of childbirth;
- be described in the appendix with naming-variant transparency.

The newborn detail is presented as two simultaneous records, not as the narrator declaring a poetic exchange of life and death.

## Japanese characters

- Routine military-police characters may be composites, clearly disclosed.
- Do not give a composite perpetrator a real historical name.
- Avoid caricatured accents, monstrous visual coding, or universal guilt.
- Keep institutional power, surveillance, vehicles, files, and weapons visible.
- The identity-exposure scene should be chilling because of ordinary speech, not sadistic performance.

## Indonesian setting and people

- Treat West Sumatra as a historically inhabited, politically dynamic society—not exotic jungle scenery.
- Include local language review for names, greetings, and social context.
- Do not use generalized “native” labels.
- Explain the post-surrender transition involving Indonesian independence and colonial return attempts without turning the game into a full political survey.

## Content notice

Before play, disclose:

- wartime occupation and political violence;
- disappearance and presumed killing;
- coercive interrogation context;
- childbirth concurrent with disappearance;
- no graphic violence.

The player can proceed, view more detail, or exit. The notice cannot spoil more than the public premise.

## Sensitivity review questions

- Does a scene make uncertainty feel like a puzzle reward?
- Does a line transfer blame from an institution to an abstract name or fate?
- Does the player gain pleasure from manipulating a vulnerable witness?
- Is a composite scene clearly labeled after completion?
- Is the wife given a life beyond waiting?
- Would an Indonesian reader recognize a social world rather than a backdrop?
- Would a Japanese reader see institutional responsibility rather than inherited ethnicity?
