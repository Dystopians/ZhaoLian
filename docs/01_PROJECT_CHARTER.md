# 01 — Project Charter

## Purpose

Create a historically responsible interactive work about Yu Dafu's disappearance that uses the affordances of games—choice, state, repetition, interfaces, and incomplete information—without turning his death into spectacle or his survival into a player optimization problem.

## Objectives

- Deliver a complete, polished, accessible static web game.
- Make the gap between an event and its later record the central mechanic.
- Offer a compelling experience to players unfamiliar with Yu Dafu while providing traceable source context for knowledgeable readers.
- Establish a reusable content pipeline for evidence-layered historical interactive fiction.

## Non-objectives

- A comprehensive biography of Yu Dafu.
- A combat, stealth, survival, or conventional detective game.
- A definitive forensic solution to the disappearance.
- A documentary that claims every line of dialogue is historical.
- A commercial live service or account-based platform.

## Audience

Primary:

- readers of modern Chinese literature;
- players of narrative and interactive-fiction games;
- museum, archive, classroom, and cultural-event audiences.

Secondary:

- researchers interested in historical representation;
- accessible-game and digital-humanities practitioners;
- overseas Chinese and Southeast Asian cultural-history audiences.

## Stakeholders

- Product owner/editor.
- Narrative designer and writer.
- Front-end/game engineer.
- Modern Chinese literature historian.
- Southeast Asian/Japanese occupation historian.
- Chinese, Japanese, and Indonesian language consultants.
- Accessibility reviewer.
- Visual and audio designers.

## Constraints

- Static-hosting architecture; no required server.
- No collection of personal data for core play.
- Primary release in zh-CN.
- Modest asset scope: 10–14 scene illustrations, 15–20 document objects, 20–25 SFX, 3–4 ambience loops.
- Historical claims require source IDs and confidence classes.
- The final disappearance remains off-screen.

## Governance

- Historical/ethical rules can veto a dramatic or technical choice.
- Product owner approves changes to fixed outcome and ending philosophy.
- Historian review is required before release candidate.
- Accessibility failures at level A/AA are release blockers unless a documented equivalent solution exists.
