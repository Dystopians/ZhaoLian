# 23 — Project Plan and Milestones

## Delivery model

A 20-week human-team plan is supplied for estimation. Codex may execute faster but must preserve milestone gates and verification evidence.

## Phase 1 — Research and design lock, weeks 1–2

Deliverables:

- claim/source register;
- historical uncertainty map;
- ethical boundaries;
- final product scope;
- technical spike for ink compilation and save restoration.

Exit gate:

- historian agrees that fixed/uncertain claims are represented correctly;
- no unresolved architecture blocker.

## Phase 2 — Paper prototype, weeks 3–4

Implement in Inky or headless ink:

- opening name field;
- “郁先生” scene;
- departure and wait sequence;
- final report and at least two endings.

Exit gate:

- player understands choices alter the record, not survival;
- wait interaction tests as intentional.

## Phase 3 — Vertical slice, weeks 5–8

Deliver a 25–35 minute polished slice with:

- core UI;
- dossier/source mode;
- sound and visual language;
- save/autosave;
- accessibility baseline;
- automated test pipeline.

Go/no-go review:

- identity, waiting, and report mechanics carry the experience without conventional branching.

## Phase 4 — Full production, weeks 9–14

- all chapters and endings;
- all evidence/claim/source data;
- interviews and two-date comparison;
- complete settings and mobile layout;
- draft art/audio.

## Phase 5 — Editorial, sound, visual and accessibility polish, weeks 15–17

- final art/audio passes;
- locked-line review;
- cultural/language consultation;
- screen-reader and keyboard passes;
- performance optimization.

## Phase 6 — Release candidate, weeks 18–20

- full historical review;
- cross-browser QA;
- release packaging and deployment;
- license/attribution audit;
- documentation and archival source bundle.
